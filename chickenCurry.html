The Ultimate Beef Burger Recipe
Beef Chicken Other Meats Vegetarian
Sweet Savoury

Feel like something warm and hearty this winter? Make sure to try this chicken curry recipe! It’s sure to warm you up.

  • Cooking Method:
  • Serves:
  • Prep Time:
  • Cooking Time:
  • Cooking
  • 8
  • 60 minutes
  • 60 minutes

    • Ingredients

    Marinade:
  • 16 fresh chicken pieces, bone in and skin on
  • 500ml (2 cups) plain double thick yoghurt
  • 5ml (1 tsp) turmeric
  • 10ml (2 tsp) paprika
  • 15ml (1 Tbsp) chicken masala
  • 3 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2cm ginger, grated
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
    • Curry Sauce
  • 30ml (2 Tbsp) butter
  • Olive oil
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 1-2 red chillies, whole (optional)
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 60ml (1/4 cup) chicken masala
  • 10ml (2 tsp) paprika
  • 60ml (1/4 cup) tomato paste/li>
  • 2 cans La Belinda chopped tomatoes
  • 1 can Food Lover’s Signature coconut cream
    • For Serving
  • Basmati rice or rotis
  • 125ml (1/2 cup) fresh coriander

    • Directions

    Marinating the Chicken:
    1. Place the chicken pieces in a non-reactive dish and season well with salt and pepper.
    2. In another bowl, combine the yoghurt, spices, garlic and ginger.
    3. Coat the chicken well with the marinade, cover, and allow to marinate in the fridge for at least 30 minutes, or overnight if you have the time.
    4. Pre-heat the oven to 180°C.
    For the Sauce:
    1. Heat a deep saucepan over medium heat.
    2. Add the butter and a dash of olive oil, once bubbling.
    3. Add the onions and fry, stirring frequently for about 8-10 minutes, or until translucent and almost caramelised.
    4. Add the whole chillies (optional), garlic and all spices.
    5. Stir constantly for 1-2 minutes for the spices to toast and gently release their flavour.
    6. Add the tomato paste and cook for about 2 more minutes.
    7. Add the chopped tomatoes and coconut cream.
    8. Cook the sauce for another 10-12 minutes.
    9. Preheat a frying pan with a dash of olive oil over a high heat.
    10. Remove the chicken from the marinade and fry each piece until well browned on both sides.
    11. Place the curry sauce with the extra marinade in a deep oven-proof dish.
    12. Place the chargrilled chicken pieces in the sauce, cover with foil and bake for about 1 hour and 30 minutes, or until soft and tender.
    13. Garnish the curry with fresh coriander and serve with basmati rice or rotis.
